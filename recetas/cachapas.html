Recipe to make cachapas

Venezuelan cachapas are the first cousins of arepas and although they are quite similar, there are substantial differences in the way they are made. Arepas are a dough that only requires pre-cooked corn flour, water and salt, while cachapas also contain cooked sweet corn kernels and are juicier.

Ingredients

  • sweet corn
  • Medium egg for the dough
  • milk
  • precooked corn flour
  • butter
  • guayanes cheese

Steps

  1. In a food processor or blender glass, mix the milk, egg and cooked sweet corn and blend well until you obtain a dough that is as smooth as possible.
  2. Add the flour and butter and blend until completely integrated and then rest the dough in a bowl covered with plastic wrap for approximately half an hour.
  3. After that time, heat a frying pan over medium heat with a little butter. When it melts, add a ladle of the dough to fill the pan.
  4. We cook it over medium heat until bubbles appear and it completely separates from the pan. With the help of a spatula we turn it over.
